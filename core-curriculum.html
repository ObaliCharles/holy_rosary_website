Core Curriculum Overview

Our curriculum follows the Ugandan National Curriculum for P.1-P.7 and is taught with local examples from Gulu and the wider Acholi sub-region. Each learning area builds strong skills and values for daily life.

Literacy & English

Focus: Phonics, reading, writing, grammar, and comprehension.

How we teach: Story reading in English and Luo, classroom libraries, and weekly reading circles.

Skills built: Clear writing, confident speaking, and strong comprehension for every learner.

Mathematics & Numeracy

Focus: Number sense, operations, measurement, and problem solving.

How we teach: Real-life examples from markets, transport, farming, and home budgeting.

Skills built: Accurate calculations, logical thinking, and practical numeracy.

Science & Environment

Focus: Living things, weather, health, and the environment.

How we teach: Simple experiments, school garden activities, and hygiene campaigns.

Skills built: Curiosity, observation, and care for the environment.

Social Studies & Civics

Focus: Community, history, geography, and citizenship.

How we teach: Local history, map work, and class discussions on rights and responsibilities.

Skills built: Respect, leadership, and community awareness.

Religious Education

Focus: Faith formation, moral values, and service.

How we teach: Prayer, scripture lessons, and community service activities.

Skills built: Compassion, integrity, and respect for others.

Creative Arts & Music

Focus: Music, dance, drama, visual art, and cultural expression.

How we teach: Performance practice, art projects, and cultural days that celebrate Acholi heritage.

Skills built: Creativity, confidence, teamwork, and cultural pride.
