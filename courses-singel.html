Academic Summary

This learning area builds number sense, problem solving, and real-world mathematics using examples from markets, farming, and daily life in Northern Uganda. Learners practice mental math, measurement, and data skills while building confidence for upper primary and beyond.

Requirements

Learners need a mathematics exercise book, ruler, pencil, and a simple set of counters or bottle tops for practice. Regular attendance and short daily practice at home are encouraged.

  • Number Sense & Place Value

  • Lecture 1.2
  • Addition and Subtraction Strategies

Using number lines and decomposition to build accurate calculations and mental math.

  • Lecture 1.3

  • 00.30.00

Learning sharing, grouping, and times tables through markets and home examples.

  • Lecture 1.4

  • 00.30.00

Working with halves, quarters, and decimals using cups, food portions, and money.

  • Geometry in Our Community
  • 00.30.00

Exploring shapes and angles found in huts, classrooms, roads, and buildings.

  • Lecture 1.6
  • Measurement & Money
  • 00.30.00

Learning length, mass, time, and Uganda shillings for budgeting and trade.

  • Lecture 1.7
  • Data and Graphs
  • 00.30.00

Collecting class data and drawing charts to interpret real-life information.

Peter Okello
 Senior Mathematics Teacher

Peter has over a decade of classroom experience and mentors teachers in practical, learner-centered numeracy. He uses local examples from markets, farming, and transport to make mathematics relevant and memorable.
